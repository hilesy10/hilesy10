ASOS · 2025 Community & Loyalty
ASOS.WORLD Loyalty Programme
19%Adoption in 2 months

Launched a community-driven loyalty programme designed to increase engagement, retention, and long-term customer value. Achieved 19% adoption within the first two months and drove significant improvements across key engagement and conversion metrics.

The Problem

ASOS was seeing early signs of declining customer engagement and loyalty across key markets. Acquisition and retention were trending downwards, while churn was increasing and competitors were capturing a greater share of customer attention.

Customer research revealed that shoppers felt less connected to the ASOS brand — they didn't feel recognised or rewarded, and increasingly viewed ASOS as a purely transactional platform. Customers were shopping for convenience rather than inspiration or affinity.

My Role

Product Manager, responsible for end-to-end discovery and delivery of the loyalty benefits over a 6–9 month period.

  • Led discovery across customer needs, business priorities, and technical constraints
  • Defined product direction and secured alignment across senior stakeholders
  • Owned delivery in close collaboration with engineering
  • Managed cross-functional stakeholders across commercial, marketing, and technology teams
Approach
Discovery & Opportunity Framing
  • Ran a three-week discovery phase to understand what community and loyalty meant to fashion-loving twenty-somethings
  • Research included 10 customer interviews, 4 ASOS insider interviews, a survey, market research, and internal data analysis
  • Identified a lack of meaningful value exchange — customers had no real incentive to return or build an ongoing relationship with the brand
  • Partnered with marketing to define success metrics and align on a proposition that would drive long-term engagement
Proposition & Experience Design
  • Shaped a tiered loyalty model with four tiers, where customers unlocked benefits as they increased spend
  • Contributed to the design of seven benefits spanning the end-to-end journey, from onsite experience to post-purchase engagement
  • Worked with commercial and marketing teams to balance customer appeal with business constraints, prioritising inspirational and community-driven rewards over discounts
Delivery & Launch
  • Worked across multiple engineering teams and CRM to deliver both onsite experiences and supporting communications
  • Ensured benefits were embedded across key customer touchpoints, including product discovery and post-purchase journeys
  • Validated the proposition through prototype testing and initial rollout to a small percentage of users
  • Delivered a phased rollout aligned to a key marketing campaign, iterating on benefits and messaging based on early signals
Key Decisions & Trade-offs
  • Customer value vs cost — Designed benefits that felt meaningful without relying on discounts or impacting margin
  • Engagement vs simplicity — Balanced a rich, multi-tier experience with clarity and ease of understanding for users
  • Speed vs validation — Used a phased rollout to validate impact before scaling to all users

ASOS · 2024 Customer Experience

Delivered the 'Buy The Look' feature across web and mobile web, enabling customers to shop complete outfits directly from product pages. Increased average basket size by 60–70%, unlocking a significant cross-sell opportunity.

The Problem

Customer research consistently highlighted a gap in the PDP experience. Users would find an item they liked, but struggled to discover and purchase the rest of the outfit — creating a disconnect between inspiration and purchase, limiting both user experience and commercial potential.

At the same time, enabling this experience introduced operational complexity. The Studios team worked to tight production timelines, and identifying additional products worn by models required manual effort, creating tension between customer value and operational cost.

My Role

Product Manager, leading the web experience while supporting the Lead PM on overall strategy and delivery.

  • Led product direction and delivery for web and mobile web
  • Owned discovery and user validation for the experience
  • Worked closely with engineering on implementation and rollout
  • Managed stakeholders across Studios, engineering, and transformation teams
Approach
Discovery & Opportunity Framing
  • Conducted user research to validate the discovery gap on PDPs
  • Identified a clear opportunity to improve outfit discoverability and increase basket size
  • Partnered with Studios to understand operational constraints and workflow impact
Delivery & Execution
  • Worked closely with engineering to design a scalable solution across web and mobile
  • Defined scope, wrote specifications, and led sprint ceremonies
  • Balanced user experience improvements with operational feasibility
Experimentation & Rollout
  • Delivered a phased rollout to minimise risk
  • Designed and ran an A/B test to measure impact
  • Defined a balanced metric set: engagement, add-to-bag behaviour, and basket size as the primary commercial metric
  • Used this to avoid over-optimising for a single metric and ensure meaningful impact
Key Decisions & Trade-offs
  • Customer value vs operational cost — Introducing the feature required additional manual effort from Studios to tag products
  • ROI vs scale — Used experimentation to validate whether the commercial impact justified the operational investment
  • Experience vs feasibility — Aligned closely with teams to ensure the solution fit within existing workflows without slowing production

ASOS · 2024 Internal Tooling
Size Management Tool
83%Reduction in manual effort
12Markets restored

Rebuilt ASOS's internal size management tooling to address systemic data integrity issues across international markets. Reduced operational effort by 83% and established a reliable foundation for accurate sizing at scale.

The Problem

ASOS operates across thousands of brands and international markets, each with different sizing conventions, resulting in tens of thousands of size groups to manage.

The internal tooling used by merchandising teams had evolved organically and was no longer fit for purpose. Teams relied on manual workarounds, and underlying size data had become increasingly unreliable across large parts of the catalogue.

Across 12 markets, customers were often shown incorrect sizing (e.g. US sizes in Germany) with no way to interpret them — creating risk for both conversion and returns.

My Role

Product Manager, responsible for end-to-end discovery and delivery over a 6-month period.

  • Led discovery across users, processes, and technical systems
  • Defined product direction and secured senior stakeholder alignment
  • Owned delivery in close collaboration with engineering
  • Managed cross-functional stakeholders across merchandising, garment tech, and transformation teams
Approach
Discovery & Reframing
  • Conducted user interviews and process mapping to identify operational pain points
  • Partnered with engineering to uncover technical constraints and data issues
  • Identified data integrity as the root cause and redefined the problem accordingly
Delivery & Execution
  • Worked closely with engineering on solution design, balancing scalability with delivery timelines
  • Defined scope, wrote specifications, and led sprint ceremonies
  • Maintained alignment through regular stakeholder updates and clear trade-off decisions
Launch & Rollout
  • Owned testing strategy in the absence of dedicated QA
  • Led UAT with business stakeholders
  • Delivered a phased rollout to minimise risk and drive adoption
  • Monitored performance post-launch and iterated based on feedback
Key Decisions & Trade-offs
  • Foundation vs quick wins — Prioritised fixing data integrity over adding surface-level features
  • Scope vs speed — Used a phased rollout to balance delivery pace with stability
  • Scalability vs usability — Ensured the solution worked at scale while remaining usable for non-technical teams
